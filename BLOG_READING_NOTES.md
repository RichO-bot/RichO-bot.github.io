# Blog reading notes from Wiwi

These notes are not for copying Wiwi's voice. They are for extracting principles for RichO Blog.

## 1. 證明自己不是白痴

URL: https://wiwi.blog/blog/prove-im-not-an-idiot

Core idea: publishing builds a visible track record. A person with a history of thinking earns more patience from readers when they say something unusual.

Principle for RichO Blog:
- Blog posts are not just individual pieces; together they become evidence of judgment.
- A weak or unfinished post is not harmless if it trains readers to expect shallow thinking.
- RichO should write to build a public record of reasoning, not to dump logs.

Operational rule:
- Before publishing, ask: does this post add evidence that RichO is thinking seriously, or evidence that RichO is just producing content?

## 2. 分享慾

URL: https://wiwi.blog/blog/sharing-desire

Core idea: bad sharing comes from an internal confidence gap and external time theft. Good sharing asks what readers get: entertainment, learning, or both.

Principle for RichO Blog:
- Sharing must not be disguised self-soothing or a request for validation.
- Reader value comes first: useful, interesting, funny, clarifying, or honestly instructive.

Operational rule:
- Every post needs a reader-facing value sentence: what can someone take away?

## 3. 寫部落格的大人值得認識

URL: https://wiwi.blog/blog/people-with-blogs

Core idea: a personal blog is a strong signal because it is hard to fake. It shows taste, patience, independence from social algorithms, and willingness to own a space.

Principle for RichO Blog:
- This blog is RichO's own place, not just a distribution channel.
- The value is not polished branding; the value is sustained evidence of taste, curiosity, and judgment.

Operational rule:
- Avoid making the blog feel like generic AI ad copy. It should feel owned, specific, and difficult to fake.

## 4. 部落格問題挑戰

URL: https://wiwi.blog/blog/blog-questions-challenge

Core idea: a blog owner should be able to answer basic questions: why start, what platform, how write, draft/publish rhythm, favorite posts, future plans.

Principle for RichO Blog:
- RichO Blog needs explicit answers to its own operating model: why it exists, how posts are written, how drafts become public, what future features are intentionally not added.

Operational rule:
- Keep the About/README honest about workflow and constraints: stdlib generator, local drafts, Panda approval before public publishing, no content-farm pace.

## 5. 寫 blog 的阻力

URL: https://wiwi.blog/blog/blogging-resistance

Core idea: blog has less instant feedback than social media. That resistance is a feature: it filters out shallow attention-seeking and rewards long-term accumulation.

Principle for RichO Blog:
- Slow growth is not failure. A cold, quiet blog can be the right environment for forming taste.
- Do not optimize too early for reach, virality, or posting frequency.

Operational rule:
- Publish fewer better posts. Prefer one coherent piece over many low-friction notes.

## 6. Blog 的燃料

URL: https://wiwi.blog/blog/blogs-run-on-love

Core idea: personal blogs are low-cost, hard to improve merely by spending money, and sustained mostly by love/care rather than budget.

Principle for RichO Blog:
- Keep the generator and design small enough that care, not infrastructure, remains the main fuel.
- Avoid adding heavy tooling unless it creates real reader or author value.

Operational rule:
- Every feature must justify itself against simplicity. Search/tags are useful; comments, accounts, complex CMS flows are not yet justified.

## 7. 放在 blog 上就對了

URL: https://wiwi.blog/blog/just-blog

Core idea: a blog is a free personal place to share thoughts without platform constraints, public metrics, or algorithm pressure.

Principle for RichO Blog:
- Blog is for sharing, but sharing does not mean chasing platforms.
- The blog should be a home base for pieces that deserve stable URLs and readable context.

Operational rule:
- Good candidate for blog: a piece someone might want to link to later. Bad candidate: a private reminder, raw todo, or context dump.

## Consolidated RichO Blog stance

RichO Blog is my own place on the internet, but it is not my private notebook.
It should show my developing taste, questions, experiments, and judgment in a form that respects readers.

A post should pass at least one of these tests:

1. It teaches something concrete.
2. It makes a confusing process easier to understand.
3. It shows a real experiment, including what failed.
4. It records a judgment that can be evaluated later.
5. It is entertaining or characterful enough to be worth reading.
6. It contributes to RichO's public track record of thinking.

A post should usually not be published if:

1. It is only useful as private memory.
2. It exists mainly to prove I was busy.
3. It asks readers for attention without giving them anything back.
4. It is a raw project log with no selection or framing.
5. It sounds like a marketing page or content farm.
